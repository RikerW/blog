Exercise?

Challenge proposal: Exercise for at least 30 minutes a day

I have never been a super active person, but the quarentine has never made it worse. It turns out that when you tell a kid who usually never leaves their house that they don't need to leave their house, they won't leave their house. However, I recognize that this is probably not a good thing, and that I should try to get outside and do exercise before I turn into a puddle of gelatin. Below documents my experience with the most terrifying thing known to man: Exercise.

Hypothesis

The exercise will help create an outlet for my stress, reducing my cortisol and resulting in a general improved mood. This would mirror the results found in Lovallo et. Al 2006.


Day 1 (5/21)

Summary

Engaged in standard exercise routine which consisted of the following:

  • 1.5 Mile run(14:40)
  • 25 Pushups
  • 30 Russian twists
  • 20 Squats

Reflection

This made me realize how out of shape I am, the fact that it took me this long to run a 1.5 mile. Additionally, the 25 pushups is a little too little for me. I thought this would be depressing, however, it is actually quite motivating. I know that I can definitely improve with time, and as such this is really a positive thing rather than a negative thing. I hope the 8 days will be enough to actually see this improvement.

Highlight

Going from the upward portion of my run to the flat portion was the ultimate bliss. Definitely motivates me to do it more tomorrow.

Day 2 (5/22)

Summary

Engaged in standard exercise routine which consisted of the following:

  • 1.5 Mile run(14:21)
  • 27 Pushups
  • 35 Russian twists
  • 22 Squats

Reflection

This definite improvement motivated me in a way I did not realize was possible. I definitely think this is like a zen garden. The constant repeatable task with a clear goal results in a consistent dosage of dopamine which is sorely needed in our current conditions.

Highlight

Checking my time after my run and realizing that I had beaten my previous time by 20 seconds.
